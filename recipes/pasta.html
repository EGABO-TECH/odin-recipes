Garlic Noodles

Description

These garlic noodles are deceivingly simple, yet devastatingly delicious. Garlic noodles were invented way back in the '70s at a restaurant in San Francisco called Thanh Long. Usually served with roasted Dungeness crab, the recipe is a very closely guarded family secret, but after lots of investigations, and even more experimentation, I think this is very close.

Ingredients

For the Secret Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 teaspoons of fish sauce
  • ¼ teaspoon of sesame oil
  • 1 pinch of cayenne pepper

For the Noodles:

  • 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 8 cloves garlic, minced
  • 6 ounces of spaghetti
  • ¼ cup of finely grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • 1 tablespoon of chopped green onion, or to taste
  • 1 pinch of red pepper flakes

Steps

  1. Step 1

    2. Gather all ingredients. Stir the soy sauce, oyster sauce, Worcestershire sauce, fish sauce, sesame oil, and cayenne pepper together in a small bowl for the secret sauce.

  2. Step 2

    3. Melt the butter in a skillet over medium heat. Add garlic; cook and stir just until fragrant, about 1 minute. Quickly stir in the secret sauce and turn off the heat.

  3. Step 3

    4. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook spahetti in the boiling water, stirring occassionally, until tender yet slightly firm to the bite, about 12 minutes.

  4. Step 4

    5. Transfer the spaghetti into the sauce using tongs, bringing some of the cooking water with it. Toss until well coated; stir in Parmesan cheese. Splash in more pasta water if noodles are too dry.

  5. Step 5

    6. Plate noodles; garnish with green onions and red pepper flakes.
